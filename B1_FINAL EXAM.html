1. Reading (400-word text)

Read the text and choose True (T) or False (F).

In recent years, the way people learn has changed dramatically. More and more individuals now choose to study online rather than in traditional classrooms. This shift happened for several reasons. First, the internet has become faster and more accessible, allowing students from all over the world to watch lessons, practise skills and complete assignments from the comfort of their own homes. Second, online courses give people the flexibility to study at their own pace. This means that busy adults with jobs or families can learn new skills without having to give up their responsibilities. For example, a working parent can study for an hour after putting their children to bed, while someone with a full-time job can learn during the weekend or whenever they have free time. However, online learning is not always easy. Many students struggle with motivation because they don’t have a teacher physically present to encourage them. Some people also find it difficult to stay focused at home when there are many distractions, such as social media, household chores or other family members. Another challenge is communication. In a real classroom, learners can ask questions immediately, but online students sometimes need to wait for answers. Even with video calls and chat systems, it can feel less interactive than speaking face-to-face. Despite these difficulties, research shows that online learning can be just as effective as traditional education if students are organised and disciplined. Many universities now offer high-quality online programmes, and companies use e-learning to train thousands of employees around the world. Another benefit is that online learning helps people develop digital skills, which are essential in today’s job market. Students learn how to use different platforms, communicate online and manage digital files. These are valuable abilities that can improve their careers. In conclusion, online learning offers many opportunities and advantages, especially for adults who need flexibility. Although it requires discipline and good time management, it allows people to continue learning throughout their lives. As technology continues to evolve, online learning will likely become even more interactive, personalised and widely used in the future.
